At most AI products, privacy is a setting — something you opt into, buried in a menu, off by default. At Audric it's the opposite: privacy isn't a setting, it's how the product is built. There's no switch to find, because there's nothing to turn on.

Here's what that means in practice.

## We collect the minimum

We collect only what's needed to run the service. There's no sign-up funnel harvesting your details — you sign in with Google and get a wallet and an account in seconds. No KYC, no seed phrase, no profile to fill out.

## Every AI request runs through a zero-retention gateway

When you send a message, it goes through a gateway that tells the model provider to process your prompt and forget it. Nothing is retained on their side, and nothing is trained on. This is on for **every** chat, by default — not a premium add-on.

## Your data is encrypted

Your chats and the files and images you create are stored encrypted and private — never on a public URL, never sold, never used to train models.

## What can be decentralized, is — and you control it

Your memory lives on decentralized storage (Walrus), encrypted and opt-in. The parts of Audric that can be yours to hold, are. That ownership is practical, not ideological: what you control can't be silently changed, read, or taken away.

---

Privacy you have to switch on is privacy someone can switch off. We'd rather build a product where that question never comes up.
